This British Museum's Bronze Age Index application, is aimed at helping the curator of Bronze Age collections catalogue a corpus of around 30,000 index cards. These cards have been collated at the British Museum for over 50 years and contain an untapped resource.

The cards have been scanned and uploaded to Flickr, so that its API can be used to retrieve and manipulate the scans. These cards hold relatively standardised data:

  • Front: Place of discovery, dimensions, description etc
  • Reverse: A line drawing of the object

What the research team would like people to do, is to transcribe these cards and enter the details from each of these into pre-determined fields and attempt to geo-reference the object via the place of discovery. By doing this, these cards can complement the data already entered into the British Museum collections management system (Merlin), but can also be uploaded to the Portable Antiquities Scheme database.
