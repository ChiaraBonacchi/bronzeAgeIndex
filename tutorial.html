British Museum Bronze Age Index transcription tutorial

Hello

This application will help the British Museum's curator of the Bronze Age, Neil Wilkin, to better understand a corpus of cards that have been collated by various scholars over the last few decades. These data will then either enhance the British Museum's collections database internally and also on their website, but data will also be uploaded to the Portable Antiquities Scheme website.

By importing all these data into the Portable Antiquities Scheme site, scholars and the public will be able to research many different object types in one single database and new research may be forthcoming.

The application is essentially very simple. When you participate, the platform will load a scanned image from the Bronze Age archive (stored on the MicroPasts Flickr stream). Once the image has been loaded, you will be asked to transcribe the contents of each card. An example of a card is shown below.

Each card has similar characteristics and we have created set fields for entering the transcribed data into. These fields are generally as follows:

  • Object type: found top centre (for example "Palstave")
  • Place of discovery or site found: top left, under heading site
  • The collection it came from: top right
  • References: centre
  • Dimensions & mass: centre left & centre right, comprising of length, width, etc.
  • Composition: centre left
  • Description: centre (sometimes just a single word, repeating object type)
  • Surface and patin: bottom left
  • Associations: centre bottom
  • Remarks: centre bottom

So, once you have filled in the fields as best as you can, all you need to do is submit the form and perhaps try another.
